Research Interests

My current research revolves around high performance distributed computing systems. In particular, the scalability of distributed systems and the use of heterogeneous resources in order to lower the energy footprint without compromising performance. The problems associated with data center resource management have always intrigued me. Other interests include unstructured distributed systems and scaling web based systems.

Master Thesis

My masters thesis titled "An Adaptive Cloud Computing Architecture for Streamlining Globally Distributed Applications" was supervised by Prof. Guillaume Pierre. In this work we envision a cloud computing infrastructure in which multiple clouds collaborate and share resources. The main focus was researching virtual machine mobility within such an environment. The target is to relocate network intensive jobs over the cloud to reduce overall network latency and bandwidth consumption. This entails studying communication patterns between compute nodes and relocating them according to heuristics.
